WARD VISITOR SYSTEM Blueprint v2
Blueprint v2 — Generic Locked Ward
Ward Visitor Management System
A self-configurable, web-based system for any locked ward. Replaces intercom and phone-based entry with a visitor kiosk, staff dashboard, and ward admin panel. Core principle: no visitor is ever left without acknowledgement.
Core visitor journey
Step 1
Visitor Arrives
Activates kiosk touchscreen at entrance door.
→
Step 2
Visitor Registers
Enters name and bay number. Selects language if needed.
→
Step 3 — Critical
Immediate Confirmation
Kiosk instantly confirms: "The ward has been notified." Visitor is never in a feedback vacuum.
→
Step 4
Staff Alerted
Dashboard shows new request. EOL and long-wait cases are visually prioritised.
→
Step 5
Staff Acts
Admits, holds, or sends a message to the kiosk. Any ward PC can respond.
→
Step 6
Door Released
Staff press physical button (now) or system triggers lock release (future). Visit logged.
Step 3 is the fundamental change. The visitor receives confirmation before any staff action has occurred.
Three components

① Visitor Kiosk

One per entrance. Tablet or small touchscreen in locked full-screen browser mode. Visitor-facing only. Inputs: name, bay number, language. Outputs: confirmation, wait messages, restricted period notices, staff messages. No patient names ever displayed.

② Staff Dashboard

Browser on any ward PC. Live queue of waiting visitors, colour-coded by wait time and priority. Any staff member can act — no login required on trusted ward network. Actions: Admit / Send Message / Hold. Shows live visitor count per bay.

③ Ward Admin Panel

For ward sister or senior staff. PIN-protected. Configure ward name, bed count, visitor rules, EOL flags, restricted periods, canned messages, and languages. No IT support needed for day-to-day use. Full access log available here.

What this solves

Current failures

✗
Visitor rings or calls — no answer — no feedback — waits indefinitely
✗
System collapses when reception unmanned (weekends, busy periods)
✗
Nurse must leave patient to open door — no resilient fallback
✗
No record of who visited, how long they waited, or who admitted them
✗
Bay capacity not systematically tracked

New system addresses

✓
Visitor always receives confirmation — immediately, before any staff action
✓
Any ward PC can receive and action a request — no single point of failure
✓
Escalating alerts highlight visitors waiting beyond threshold
✓
Full timestamped log — who arrived, waited, was admitted, and by whom
✓
Bay capacity enforced automatically
Generalisability

Designed to deploy on any locked ward. Each ward configures its own instance: ward name, bed count, visitor limits, entrance count, canned messages, languages, and restricted period rules. The HDU/ITU deployment is the first use case but the system does not depend on it.

System Logic
Rules & Logic
How the system behaves in each scenario. Based on confirmed requirements — flagged items need further input before build.
Admission rules
①
Maximum 2 visitors per bay. System checks live count before admitting. If full, visitor sees: "There are already 2 visitors at Bay X. You will be admitted when one leaves." Enforced at system level.
②
Restricted periods block new admissions. Kiosk shows restricted period screen. Visitors can still register and queue. When period ends, queued visitors are presented to staff in order of arrival.
③
EOL bays are highest priority. Always shown at top of queue regardless of wait time. Staff exercise their own judgement — the system surfaces the request prominently, it does not auto-admit.
④
No visitor is ever left without acknowledgement. Kiosk confirms receipt immediately on submission, before any staff action.
⑤
Any staff member can act on any request. Dashboard on all ward PCs, no login barrier, shared queue.

Restricted period logic
·
Staff activate any restricted period manually via admin panel — ward rounds, handover, or emergency closure.
·
Kiosk immediately switches to restricted period screen with estimated resumption time, or "time not yet known" if not set.
·
Visitors already on the ward during a restricted period — staff continue to manage departures verbally, as at present. The system does not attempt to replace this.
·
When period ends, staff clear the flag. Queued visitors are presented to staff in order of arrival.

Privacy note
The system stores visitor names and bay numbers only. No patient names, dates of birth, or clinical information are held. This minimises personal data exposure and should be reviewed by the trust's information governance team before deployment.
